# PAIN-MATRIX.md

Buyer-out pain ranking per segment. Validated against three independent deep research reports (Gemini, Perplexity, ChatGPT Pro) on 2026-05-13. This is the strategic foundation for the v4 copy reframes.

## Methodology

Each segment's pain points are ranked by:

1. **Business impact** — what it costs them in revenue, time, or risk
2. **Buyer-articulation** — does the operator verbalise this pain in their own language, or is it a consultant-projected concern?
3. **Walkthrough fit** — does our solution touch this pain meaningfully, or are we forcing a fit?

The principle: if our solution solves problem #4 brilliantly while the buyer is preoccupied with problems #1-3, we lose. We commit to segments where our solution addresses a top-3 pain.

## Phase 1 segments

### Off-plan developers — STRONG fit

| Rank | Pain | Walkthrough leverage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Remote buyer trust for an unbuilt asset | Direct hit. The show flat walkthrough closes the trust gap. Research shows reservation timelines collapse from 112 days to 14 when buyers can virtually walk the unit. |
| 2 | Pre-sale targets, construction finance, early-buyer discounting | Indirect hit. Faster pre-sales unlock bank drawdowns. Walkthrough accelerates pre-sales. |
| 3 | Delay, specification, legal and delivery-risk management | No fit. Walkthrough doesn't help. |

**Strategic implication:** Lead the page with sales velocity / remote trust framing. The Dubai-buyer reach angle is a supporting detail, not the headline.

### Wedding venues — STRONG fit (for remote couple confidence)

| Rank | Pain | Walkthrough leverage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Lead quality and ghosting | Indirect. Walkthrough does not fix ghosting directly but reduces unqualified enquiries by letting couples self-screen. Pricing transparency is the upstream filter. |
| 2 | Pricing, package transparency, budget mismatch | Indirect. Pricing-anchor block on the page addresses this. |
| 3 | Remote confidence in the estate, wedding flow, and logistical plan | Direct hit. This is what the walkthrough does. |
| 4 | Seasonality and date yield | No fit. |

**Strategic implication:** Keep the strong "Let her walk the chapel" hero. Add a pricing-transparency block to address the upstream ghosting pain. Sharpen outcome cards to remove overclaim language.

### Boutique hotels — MEDIUM-STRONG fit (for OTA commission recovery)

| Rank | Pain | Walkthrough leverage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Labour, cost, service pressure | No fit. Walkthrough doesn't solve staffing. |
| 2 | OTA dependence, direct-booking leakage, loss of guest control | Direct hit. Walkthrough enables direct-booking conversion. |
| 3 | Manual pricing and weak revenue management | No fit. |
| 4 | Differentiated guest experience and proof of "why us" | Indirect hit. Walkthrough provides differentiation. |

**Strategic implication:** Keep the "Recover the commission OTAs take" hero — it hits the validated #2 pain head-on, which is real and quantifiable. Sharpen outcome cards to direct-booking economics. Do not try to address #1 (labour) because the walkthrough doesn't solve that.

### STR property managers — MEDIUM fit (for owner-facing professionalism)

| Rank | Pain | Walkthrough leverage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Owner trust, owner retention, owner acquisition | Indirect. Walkthrough is part of the owner-trust toolkit (proof of investment, professional presentation) but not a churn lever. |
| 2 | Operational reliability at scale (cleaning, maintenance, inspections) | No fit. |
| 3 | Demand pressure, channel dependence, revenue management | Indirect. Walkthrough helps direct-booking lift but doesn't solve channel dependence at root. |

**Strategic implication:** Reframe the page from supplier-side ("fleet pricing") to owner-facing ("show owners what they pay you for"). The bookings/ADR lift becomes outcome cards, not the hero claim. Honest framing: walkthrough is part of the toolkit, not the silver bullet.

### Galleries, cultural venues, museums — MEDIUM fit (for venue hire + grant evidence)

| Rank | Pain | Walkthrough leverage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Funding instability, deficits, staff capacity | Indirect. Walkthrough as grant evidence strengthens funding applications. |
| 2 | Attendance, audience relevance, competition for attention | Indirect. The walkthrough is a "preview the experience" tool that supports visitor decisions. |
| 3 | Collector/patron pipeline (commercial galleries only) | Indirect for commercial galleries. Out of scope for v4 (institutional focus). |
| 4 | Fragmented teams, disconnected technology | No fit. |

**Strategic implication:** Split the segment. v4 page focuses on institutional/museum/cultural-foundation buyers. Lead with venue hire (new revenue line) and grant evidence (funding leverage). The exhibition-archive frame is the existing strong hero.

## Demoted segments

### Medical tourism clinics — FORCED FIT

| Rank | Pain | Walkthrough leverage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Clinical trust, credentials, outcomes, perceived risk | Indirect at best. Walkthrough alone doesn't establish clinical competence. |
| 2 | Lead response speed and cross-border coordination | No fit. |
| 3 | Hidden costs, aftercare, complications | No fit. |

**Strategic implication:** Demote to inbound-only. The walkthrough cannot be the decision driver. Future reframe would need to be credential-anchored ("clinical transparency walkthrough alongside credentials and aftercare proof"), but this is Phase 2 work, not v4.

### Solo Airbnb hosts — FORCED FIT

| Rank | Pain | Walkthrough leverage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Bookings, visibility, platform dependence | Marginal. Walkthrough helps differentiation but is downstream of algorithm. |
| 2 | Cleaning, turnover, cleaning-fee conversion risk | No fit. |
| 3 | Damage, deposits, guest screening | No fit. |
| 4 | Guest communication, expectation management | Marginal. Walkthrough can reduce surprise reviews. |

**Strategic implication:** Demote to inbound-only. Unit economics fail after CAC. Page exists for SEO and inbound but no acquisition spend.

### Design-led restaurants — REAL BUT NARROW FIT

| Rank | Pain | Walkthrough leverage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Capturing private hire and event revenue systematically | Direct hit (for venues with meaningful private hire). |
| 2 | Labour and food cost pressure | No fit. |
| 3 | Traffic volatility, weak owned demand | No fit. |
| 4 | Event execution complexity | Marginal. |

**Strategic implication:** Phase 2 hold. Fit is real for the top-tier design-led private-hire venues. Addressable pool is small (~15-25 venues in Tbilisi). Sector cash-strapped even at the design-led tier. Revisit after Phase 1 traction.

## Cross-segment unifying frame

Three research reports converge on the same meta-frame:

**"Remote-buyer confidence for high-consideration spaces."**

Or: **the trust layer between distance and commitment.**

The hub H1 "They are somewhere else. Win them anyway." already names this frame. The v4 segment pages follow through harder by addressing the specific commercial outcome enabled by closing the distance gap for each buyer type.

Operator-articulation patterns across segments:

- "Buyers wire money for something they cannot stand inside" (developers)
- "Couples decide between six venues without flying out" (weddings)
- "Guests book direct on our site or hedge to Booking" (hotels)
- "Owners ask what we are doing to market their unit" (managers)
- "Curators decide from documentation, not photographs" (galleries)

Each of these is the same structural pain — buyer is somewhere else and needs to walk the space before they commit — applied to a specific commercial decision.

## What we do not claim

We do not claim the walkthrough solves:

- Staffing or labour cost (hotels, restaurants)
- Cleaning, maintenance, turnover (STR managers, solo hosts)
- Owner churn directly (STR managers — we support the workflow, not the lever)
- Clinical trust (medical clinics)
- Funding instability (galleries — we strengthen one input)
- Regulatory risk (any segment)
- Foreign-buyer legal / payments (developers)

These constraints are honest. Overclaiming on segments where the fit is forced damages trust faster than not addressing those buyers at all.
